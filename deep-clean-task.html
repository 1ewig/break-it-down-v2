Deep clean
the kitchen

There is no rush. Every little bit helps. You don't have to do this all at once — even one step today is real progress.

Gentle Progress
0 of 5 steps 0%

Take your time. Pause whenever you need to.

Bring a trash bag into the kitchen
The only thing you need to do right now
Tiny step 1× Trash bag ~1 min

Don't worry about filling it yet. Just putting the bag in the room is a massive win. You're setting your future self up for success.

Why this matters

Having a bag nearby removes the #1 friction point for clearing clutter. You're removing a barrier, not doing a chore.

Clear the countertops
Move things off — sorting comes later
Small step 5–10 min

You don't need to decide where things go. Just move everything to one end or onto the table. Decisions come later — this step is just about clearing space.

If something doesn't have an obvious home, place it in a "decide later" pile. You're not obligated to sort right now.
Why this matters

A clear surface is both physically and mentally easier to wipe down — and it makes the kitchen feel dramatically lighter right away.

Wipe down the countertops
One surface at a time is fine
Small step 1× Cloth or sponge 5 min

A damp cloth is enough. You don't need a special cleaner. Wipe in slow, gentle sweeps — this isn't a race.

Start with the section nearest to you. Finish that, then decide if you want to continue. It's okay to stop after one counter.
Why this matters

Clean surfaces are where cooking feels pleasant and easy again. Even one wiped counter shifts the whole mood of the kitchen.

Clean the sink
Dishes first, or just the basin — your call
Moderate step 10–20 min

If dishes feel like too much right now, it's completely fine to just rinse and wipe the basin. A clean sink has a surprisingly big impact on how the kitchen feels.

Dishes can wait in a neat stack. The goal is a sink that feels usable, not perfect.
Why this matters

The sink is the heart of the kitchen. Clearing it tends to make everything else feel more manageable.

Sweep or vacuum the floor
Just the middle — edges are optional
Small step 5 min

You don't need to move appliances or get into corners. A quick pass through the main walkable area is genuinely enough for today.

Why this matters

A swept floor is the finishing touch that makes your earlier work feel complete and cohesive. It ties the room together.
